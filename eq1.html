Multiple Choice Questions

1. The atomic number of an element is equal to the number of:

  • (a) Protons
  • (b) Protons and electrons
  • (c) Neutrons
  • (d) Neutrons and electrons

2. The element with the smallest atomic radius is:

  • (a) Sodium
  • (b) Magnesium
  • (c) Fluorine
  • (d) Helium

3. Which particle has no charge?

  • (a) Proton
  • (b) Neutron
  • (c) Electron
  • (d) Nucleus

4. The mass of an electron is approximately:

  • (a) 9.109 × 10-31 kg
  • (b) 1.673 × 10-27 kg
  • (c) 1.675 × 10-27 kg
  • (d) 1.007 amu

5. Which element has the highest ionization energy?

  • (a) Lithium
  • (b) Carbon

  • (d) Helium

6. Electrons are found in:

  • (a) The nucleus
  • (b) Orbitals
  • (c) Protons
  • (d) Neutrons

7. The relative atomic mass of an element is the weighted average of the masses of:

  • (a) Its isotopes
  • (b) Its ions
  • (c) Its protons and neutrons
  • (d) Its molecules

8. The number of moles in 12 grams of carbon is:

  • (a) 1 mole

  • (d) 6 moles

  • (a) Magnesium

  • (a) Rutherford
  • (b) Bohr
  • (c) Schrödinger

True/False

1. All isotopes of a given element have the same number of protons. True

2. The electron has a positive charge. False

3. The mass of an electron is negligible compared to the mass of a proton. True

4. Rutherford’s model of the atom could not explain the stability of an atom. True

5. Bohr’s model of the atom was based on classical mechanics. False

6. Electrons in the same orbital must have opposite spins. True

7. The p orbital is spherical in shape. False

8. All atoms of a given element are identical in mass. False

9. An ion is an atom or molecule with a net electric charge. True

10. The nucleus of an atom contains electrons. False

  • (a) Hydrogen - H
  • (b) Carbon - C
