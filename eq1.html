Equation 1:

(1 + i)-5 × (1 + 1/i)5 = 32

This equation involves raising the complex numbers to the power of 5 and multiplying the results. The first term is (1 + i) raised to the power of -5. The second term is (1 + 1/i) raised to the power of 5. Simplifying 1/i gives -i, which transforms the second term to (1 - i). Solving this gives us the final result equal to 32.

Equation 2:

(1 - i)n × (1 - 1/i)n = 2n

In this equation, the base (1 - i) and (1 - 1/i) are both raised to the power of n. As in the previous case, 1/i is equivalent to -i, simplifying the second term to (1 + i). The multiplication of both expressions results in 2n.

Equation 3:

(1 - 1/i)4 × (1 - 1/i)4 = -8

Here, both terms are (1 - 1/i), which simplifies to (1 + i). Each of these terms is raised to the power of 4, and their product results in -8. The negative value comes from the powers of complex numbers, particularly when dealing with imaginary units.

Key Notes:

  • In these equations, i represents the imaginary unit, where i2 = -1.
  • The simplification of terms involving 1/i results in the transformation of the terms into expressions with i or -i.
  • The powers of complex numbers follow the rules of exponents and can be simplified using De Moivre's Theorem.
